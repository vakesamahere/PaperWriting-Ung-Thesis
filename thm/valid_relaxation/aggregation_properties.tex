\paragraph*{Properties of $\Phi_{\arcSet}$.}
Because $\Phi_{\arcSet}$ is a function, the family $\{\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)\}_{a\in\arcSet}\cup\{\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(\bot)\}$ forms a partition of $\full{\arcSet}$.
Furthermore, every service arc $a'\in\full{\flightArc}$ maps to a unique element of $\flightArc$. Hence
\[
    \full{\flightArc} = \biguplus_{a\in\flightArc}\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a).
\]
For holding arcs we obtain the disjoint decomposition
\[
    \full{\holdingArc} = \left(\biguplus_{a\in\holdingArc}\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)\right) \uplus \Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(\bot),
\]
where $\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(\bot)$ comprises the short ground waits that are entirely absorbed at a single partial time node.
The node mapping $\Phi_{\nodeSet}$ enjoys the same partition property: $\full{\nodeSet} = \biguplus_{(i,t)\in\nodeSet} \Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(i,t)$.

\paragraph*{Compatibility of pre-images.}
Fix $a=((i,t)\rightarrow(j,t'))\in\arcSet$ and let $a'=((i,\tau)\rightarrow(j,\tau'))$ be an arc in the full network.
By the definition of $\Phi_{\arcSet}$ we have
\[
    a'\in\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)
    \iff 
    \Phi_{\nodeSet}((i,\tau))=(i,t)\ \text{and}\ \Phi_{\nodeSet}((j,\tau'))=(j,t').
\]
Thus the tail of $a'$ automatically lies in $\Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(i,t)$ and its head in $\Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(j,t')$, and conversely any arc leaving $(i,\tau)\in\Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(i,t)$ and arriving at $(j,\tau')\in\Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(j,t')$ must map to $a$ (rather than to $\bot$).
Equivalently,
\[
    a\in\outArcSet{(i,t)},\ a'\in\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)
    \iff \exists (i,\tau)\in\Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(i,t)\ \text{s.t.}\ a'\in\outArcSet{(i,\tau)},
\]
and similarly
\[
    a\in\inArcSet{(j,t')},\ a'\in\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)
    \iff \exists (j,\tau')\in\Phi_{\nodeSet}^{-1}(j,t')\ \text{s.t.}\ a'\in\inArcSet{(j,\tau')}.
\]
This compatibility allows us to swap sums between partial arcs and their full-network pre-images.

In particular, since the pre-image family $\{\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)\}_{a\in\flightArc}$ constitutes a mathematically strict partition of the full service arc set $\full{\flightArc}$, the Law of Total Summation holds. For any real-valued parameters or variables $y_{a'}$ indexed by the full service arcs $a'\in\full{\flightArc}$, we have:
\[
    \sum_{a\in\flightArc}\ \sum_{a'\in\Phi_{\arcSet}^{-1}(a)} y_{a'} 
    = \sum_{a'\in\full{\flightArc}} y_{a'}.
\]
