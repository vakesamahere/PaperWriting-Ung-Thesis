\MakeAbstract{
服务网络设计问题广泛存在于航空、铁路、城际客运等定时服务运营场景中。经典服务网络设计将需求视为外生给定，在时空网络上优化服务调度和资源分配；然而实际中顾客会根据服务属性进行选择，使需求随服务计划内生变化。将选择行为纳入服务网络设计引入了选择变量与资源调度约束之间的强耦合，使混合整数规划模型在实际规模下难以求解。
为此，本文提出行程聚合与动态细化框架（\algoNotation）。该框架利用时空网络聚合诱导的路径签名将原始行程表示为超级行程集合，并为每个超级行程构造可兼容原始行程组合吸引力总和的上界，既覆盖原问题中的可行选择组合，又抑制时间聚合导致的不可实现收入。基于超级行程表示，本文构造原问题最优值的双侧界：在聚合网络上求解松弛模型获得下界，利用频率结构和超级行程收入约束在完整网络上修复得到可行上界。当双侧界尚不能证明最优性时，有针对性地更新离散化及超级行程表示，保证算法有限收敛到全局最优解。在真实航空排班实例上的计算实验表明，超级行程聚合将候选行程从142万条压缩至约21万条（减少85\%），\algoNotation 在所有测试实例上均于5小时内达到低于1\%的最优性间隙，显著优于直接求解方法。

\MakeAbstractEng{
Service network design with customer choice (\problemName) is prevalent in airlines, railways, intercity passenger transport, and other scheduled service operations. Classical service network design treats demand as exogenously given and optimizes service scheduling and resource assignment on discretized time-space networks; however, in real service systems, customers choose among available service options based on departure times, connection quality, travel time, and fares, making demand endogenous to the service plan. Incorporating choice behavior into service network design captures supply-demand interactions more accurately, but introduces strong coupling among large-scale choice variables, choice-consistency constraints, and resource scheduling constraints, rendering the resulting mixed-integer program intractable at realistic scales. Traditional approaches based on time-space network discretization or dynamic refinement primarily compress the physical service network and cannot address the computational challenges arising from the explosion of the customer choice space and the unrealizable revenue caused by temporal aggregation.

To this end, this paper proposes an Itinerary Aggregation and Dynamic Refinement framework (\algoNotation). The framework uses path signatures induced by physical time-space network aggregation to represent the original itinerary set as a compact set of super-itineraries. Unlike simple merging of original itineraries, this paper computes a tight upper bound on the combined attraction of feasible original itinerary combinations represented by each super-itinerary, and constructs corresponding revenue bounds. This aggregation rule ensures that super-itineraries cover all feasible choice combinations in the original problem, preventing the relaxation model from underestimating feasible choices; it also prevents simple accumulation of revenues from incompatible original itineraries within the same aggregated choice set, thereby suppressing unrealizable revenue caused by temporal aggregation. Based on this super-itinerary representation, the paper constructs two-sided bounds on the optimal value of the original problem: on one hand, solving a compact relaxation model on the aggregated time-space network and super-itinerary space yields a valid lower bound; on the other hand, using the service frequency structure from the relaxation solution together with revenue-layer constraints derived from super-itineraries strengthens a feasible-solution repair model on the full time-space network, yielding feasible upper bounds. When the two-sided bounds cannot yet certify optimality of the current feasible solution, the paper updates the time-space discretization and its induced super-itinerary representation in a targeted manner, and proves that the algorithm converges to the globally optimal solution in a finite number of iterations. Computational experiments on real-world airline scheduling instances demonstrate that super-itinerary aggregation compresses 1.43 million candidate itineraries to approximately 210 thousand (an 85\% reduction), and \algoNotation\xspace achieves less than 1\% optimality gap within 5 hours on all test instances, significantly outperforming direct solution methods.
}{Service Network Design, Customer Choice, Itinerary Aggregation, Mixed-integer Programming, Exact Algorithm}
